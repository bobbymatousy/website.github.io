FAQ

Common diligence questions we receive from buyers and partners.

What do you mean by “intermediate crops”?

Intermediate crops are grown within an existing rotation framework and can support additional supply without competing directly with primary food crops. Program design depends on local agronomy and grower economics.

Can you provide certified material today?

We don’t claim certifications on this demo site. Our approach is designed to be certification-ready where applicable (e.g., ISCC/CORSIA), subject to partner scope and project-specific requirements.

What volumes can you supply?

Volumes are scoped per geography and season and depend on grower participation and routing capacity. We typically discuss volume bands after aligning on requirements and timeline.

Where do you operate?

We operate across Germany, Bucharest (Romania), and Kazakhstan. Specific origin and delivery routing is defined project-by-project.

How do you handle traceability?

We design chain-of-custody and reporting expectations into contracting, batching, and partner handoffs. The exact documentation package is aligned with buyer requirements.

What is your contracting approach?

We use structured contracting with clear specifications, delivery windows, and responsibilities across growers, aggregators, logistics, and processing partners. Terms vary by corridor and partner model.

What timelines should buyers expect?

Timelines depend on seasonality and build-out requirements. We typically align early on intended delivery windows and the data package needed for downstream compliance.
